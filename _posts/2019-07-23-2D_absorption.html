--- title: Reducing the spatial aliasing error using the non-reflecting layer ---


Adding non-reflecting layer can help reduce the spatial aliasing error by absorbing the energy at the boundaries. But if the absorption is too strong, the acoustic field will become inaccurate due to excess absoprtion. On the other hand, if the absoprtion is too small, then the aliasing error cannot be reduced. In this case, the effect of non-reflecting layer is investigated by setting up different medium.NRL_gamma and medium.NRL_alpha.

2D forward simulation

This simulation setup is based on the Simulation of a 2D homogeneous medium using the frequency-specific mixed domain method. Computational domain and simulation setup are the same in the two cases. In this case, three pairs of medium.NRL_gamma and medium.NRL_alpha are studied and they are: medium.NRL_gamma=0.1 and medium.NRLb_alpha=0.5, medium.NRL_gamma=0.5 and medium.NRL_alpha=0.2, and medium.NRL_gamma=0.8 and medium.NRL_alpha=0.05. The pressure distribution along the central axia simulated at different absorption layer configurations are shown in the figure below. For each simulation case, coefficients for the absorption layer should be selected carefully.
